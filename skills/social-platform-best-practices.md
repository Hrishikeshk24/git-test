# Social Platform Best Practices Skill

## Instructions

Adapt content for each platform using platform-specific engagement tactics and best practices.

## LinkedIn Strategy

**Optimal Structure:**
1. **Hook** (first line) - Bold claim, surprising stat, or question
2. **Context** - Story or insight related to hook
3. **Insight/Data** - Specific example or metric
4. **CTA** - Clear next step (comment, share, learn more)

**LinkedIn Best Practices:**
- Length: 150-300 words (sweet spot for engagement)
- Emojis: 1-2 max (at strategic points)
- Hashtags: 3 maximum, relevant to industry
- Format: Line breaks between sections for scannability
- Timing: Post Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am

**LinkedIn Hooks That Work:**
- "I spent 6 months researching... here's what we found"
- "95% of teams struggle with X. Here's why."
- "We tried the conventional approach and failed. Here's what worked instead."
- Question-based: "What's the biggest challenge in your industry?"

**Example LinkedIn Post:**
```
Hard truth: Most teams waste 40+ hours/month on messaging alignment.

We analyzed 100+ marketing teams and found the same pattern:
- Inconsistent voice across channels
- Multiple tools, zero integration
- Brand guidelines that nobody remembers

Then we built a different approach. Instead of more tools, we created a system that embeds brand voice directly into the workflow.

The result: Our customers cut messaging time from 6 weeks to 3 days.

What's your biggest messaging challenge? (Comment below)

#MarketingAutomation #BrandStrategy #ContentTeams
```

## Twitter/X Strategy

**Optimal Structure:**
1. **Hook** - Punchy, witty opening (first 22 chars critical)
2. **Core message** - One clear idea
3. **Proof point** - Stat, example, or observation
4. **CTA** - Retweet, reply, follow, link

**Twitter Best Practices:**
- Length: 120-150 characters (leaves room for retweets)
- Tone: Witty but professional
- Emojis: 1-2 for personality
- Thread potential: Each tweet should stand alone
- Timing: Post during peak hours (9am, 12pm, 5pm)

**Twitter Hooks That Work:**
- Contrarian takes: "Everyone does X. That's wrong."
- Data-driven: "New research shows..."
- Question-based: "What if X was actually Y?"
- Emotional: "We built X so nobody has to suffer through Y."

**Example Tweets:**
```
Everyone thinks faster tools = better marketing output.
They're wrong. Better systems = consistent quality.

That's why we built AI that enforces brand voice at every step.

---

Watched a team write the exact same sentence
3 different ways for 3 different channels.
15 minutes wasted.

Multiply that by 50 posts/month.
That's 12+ hours of wasted time.

We solved this. Here's how →
```

## Instagram Strategy

**Optimal Structure:**
1. **Caption Hook** - First line must grab (Instagram cuts off after ~55 chars with "more")
2. **Story** - Behind-the-scenes or relatable angle
3. **Insight** - Takeaway or lesson learned
4. **CTA** - Link in bio, DM, follow, save

**Instagram Best Practices:**
- Caption length: 150-300 words
- Hashtags: 8-12 relevant tags (end of caption)
- Emojis: 3-5 for personality and visual breaks
- Format: Spaces and line breaks for readability
- Content types: Mix education (50%), entertainment (30%), inspiration (20%)

**Instagram Hooks That Work:**
- Behind-the-scenes: "Here's what we built today..."
- Relatable problem: "This happens to every marketing team..."
- Surprising stat: "Only X% of teams..."
- Question-based: "What's this feature for? 👇"

**Example Instagram Caption:**
```

We watched a team spend 3 weeks writing product launch copy.
100 versions.
7 feedback rounds.
Still not on-brand.

We built our platform specifically to solve this:
✓ Brand voice embedded in every prompt
✓ Intelligent suggestions (not templates)

Now the same launch takes 3 days.

What would you build if you had 40+ hours back?

Link in bio to see how it works 🔗

#MarketingTech #BrandStrategy #ContentCreation #MarketingAutomation
```

## Facebook Strategy

**Optimal Structure:**
1. **Conversational Hook** - Warm, human opening
2. **Relatable Problem** - Something your audience experiences
3. **Story or Data** - Evidence that builds credibility
4. **Soft CTA** - Engagement-focused, not sales-y

**Facebook Best Practices:**
- Length: 200-500 words
- Tone: Community-focused, conversational
- Format: Casual, friendly (more relaxed than LinkedIn)
- Emojis: 2-4 for warmth
- CTA: Engagement (comments, shares) before link clicks

**Facebook Hooks That Work:**
- Community-building: "Hey marketing team members, quick question..."
- Relatable: "Does this happen in your workflow too?"
- Story-based: "This morning, a customer told us..."
- Celebration: "We hit X milestone and we couldn't have done it without..."

**Example Facebook Post:**
```
Hey marketing team 👋

re-writing the same messaging for different channels?

We asked 200+ teams this and got surprising answers:

The root problem? Tools built for individuals, not teams.
And no enforcement of brand voice across the workflow.

We decided to build something different.
A system that helps teams move faster
WHILE maintaining perfect brand alignment.

The results have been incredible:
Our customers cut messaging time in half.
And paradoxically, the output quality improved.

(Better systems → better results)

What's your biggest messaging battle?
Drop a comment below. Would love to hear what you're dealing with.

P.S. If you want to see it in action, we're doing demos this week. Happy to chat.
```

## Cross-Platform Adaptation Rules

| Element | LinkedIn | Twitter | Instagram | Facebook |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Tone** | Professional | Witty | Casual | Community-focused |
| **Length** | 150-300 words | 120-150 chars | 150-300 words | 200-500 words |
| **Emojis** | 1-2 | 1-2 | 3-5 | 2-4 |
| **Hashtags** | 3 | 2-3 | 8-12 | 3-5 |
| **Hook Style** | Story/insight | Witty/data | Question/behind-scenes | Relatable/question |
| **CTA** | Direct | Engagement | Engagement then link | Engagement first |
| **Timing** | Weekday morning | Multiple daily | Evening | Evening |

## Unified Brand Requirements

Across ALL platforms, maintain:
- Consistent brand voice and personality
- Same key messages and positioning
- Authentic representation of company values
- High-quality, professional presentation

**But adapt the delivery for each platform's culture.**
